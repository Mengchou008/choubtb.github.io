ផ្សារណាត់បាត់ដំបង

Nat Market is a large market located in the center of Battambang city,
located across the main roads such as Street 1, Street 2 and Street 3 and the front facing Stung Sangke.
This market was built in 1936 in the style of Art Deco (Art Deco), which is popularly known as a heritage building.

Within the framework of urban planning in the Indochina region under its control,
France sent a well-known architect: Ernest Ephraim came to Indochina.
This is according to the document on the renovation of Central Market in Phnom Penh.

When Ernest Ephraim, an expert in archeology and urban planning, arrived in
Indochina, he was in charge of the Hanoi School of Fine Arts. Vietnam since 1921.
The same document states that this architect rejected the traditional Khmer architectural style in the construction.
Market in modern Indochinese style, with proper sanitation and hygiene.
